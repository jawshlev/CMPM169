Experiment 2: Vector Art, Animation & Interactivity

Description

With this experiment I aimed to create a somewhat audioreactive, line render with blob tracking. The original image was of graffiti that I saw when I was in Naples, and a photo of my mouth after a brutal mosh pit. The image is also being distorted.

Technical

I used audio input to affect the speed of the line renderer, and the blob tracking. I then used Gausian noise to render the line's bizzare shape, and used the same noise to distort the image. I used the RGBA values from the Gausian noise to create those weird numbers at each of the line segments. They look like alien language because of the font I used. For the numbers within the blob render I used the dataset provided by the blob render node. The entire project was made in TouchDesigner.

Reflection

It was a bit difficult deciding exactly what I wanted for the background image. I wanted it to be unique and I wanted to make the project my own as much as I could. I take a lot of photos when I travel and stumbled upon that image of the angel, I was inspired by these rough collages I see on my Pinterest that featured related images crudely stacked upon one another. I spent a long while with the technical aspects of the project because I wanted the line segment to reflect what I thought a biblically accurate angel's halo would look like. I spend awhile deciding on what font I wanted for the line segment also on the colors. Bringing the video to p5.js was not too much of a problem, however, I did encounter issues with the resolution.
